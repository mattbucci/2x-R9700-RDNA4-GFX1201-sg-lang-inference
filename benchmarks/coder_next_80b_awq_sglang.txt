Qwen3-Coder-Next-80B AWQ-4bit Benchmark
========================================
Engine:   SGLang v0.5.10 + RDNA4 patches
Hardware: 2x AMD Radeon AI PRO R9700 (gfx1201), TP=2
Weights:  ~/AI/models/Qwen3-Coder-Next-AWQ (community AWQ, 47GB)
          23.14 GB/GPU, ~6 GB free for KV + SSM state
Config:   --context-length 8192 --kv-cache-dtype fp8_e4m3
          --disable-cuda-graph --attention-backend triton
Date:     2026-04-11

Model: 80B total / 3B active. 512 experts (10 active per token).
       DeltaNet linear attention + 12 full attention layers.
       Only MoE experts quantized (AWQ 4-bit); DeltaNet+attention weights BF16.

Notes:
- Throughput flat ~25 tok/s: VRAM-limited to effectively 1 concurrent
  (23 GB model weights leaves only ~6 GB for KV + SSM state)
- DeltaNet decode is near-constant TPOT regardless of context length
- Context length mainly affects prefill time, not decode speed
- Quality: excellent on code tasks
- DeltaNet layers intentionally kept BF16 — INT4 quantization destroys
  recurrent state quality (S(t) = g*S(t-1) + delta accumulates noise)
- REAM variant (cyankiwi) prunes 80B->60B, saves 25% VRAM

Performance analysis:
- Weight reads per decode token: ~3.7 GB
    DeltaNet BF16 (36 layers): ~2.4 GB (64%)
    MoE 10-active AWQ (48 layers): ~0.8 GB (22%)
    Shared expert BF16 (48 layers): ~0.3 GB (8%)
    Attention BF16 (12 layers): ~0.2 GB (6%)
- BF16 DeltaNet weight reads dominate — this is the architectural speed limit
- llama.cpp Vulkan achieves 79 tok/s by quantizing everything (GGUF Q4_K_M)
  but at the cost of DeltaNet quality degradation
